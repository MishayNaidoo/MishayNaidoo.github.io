About

Now, almost four years and roughly a trillion exams later I can once again confidently say:

I really hate writing exams...

Whilst my disdain for exams remains resolute, I have fallen in love with engineering. My lecturers in this degree seemed to have a knack for throwing projects and assignments at me that I felt I was totally incapable of doing and proceeding to tell me to go ahead and do them anyway. This in turn, taught me how to approach projects that seemed impossible at the start with a new confidence and belief that I could get them done. With this attitude, I have gone on to pursue and accomplish various projects that this website is here to show off to you. However, my blind confidence hasn't always been enough and there are one of two failed projects. Unfortunately, you'll read about those on here too.

Projects

Project #1

Rowdy Chess V1

An automated chessboard myself and two friends created, that uses a 3D printed XY plotter to pick up and move chesspieces. This board enabled players to make chess moves using only voice commands.

Python, CAD, 3D Printing

Project #2

Disaster Drones

Currently, four of us have entered into a competition hosted by Mathworks where each team must design a control algorithm to solve a maze using a line following drone. This is something we are currently working on and the tournament is still in progress.

MATLab, Simulink, Control Systems Engineering

Project #3

Perch Scale

A smart perch designed to weigh Yellow-Billed Hornbills in their natural habitat without causing any distrubances to their environment. This was designed to aid researchers at the FitzPatrick institute in their research of the birds. The project used a load cell and ESP32 Cam microcontrollers to determine the bird's weight and detect the bird using a camera. The system was also solar powered.

3D Printing, CAD, Embedded Systems

Project #4

Line Following Robot

This project involved designing a control algorithm that solved a maze using a two wheeled robot. The algorithm was created in Matlab Simulink and ran on a microcontroller. We also designed a light colour sensor to determine the colour of a traffic light and were able to make the robot stop moving at a red light and start at a green one.

Matlab, Simulink, Embedded Systems, Analog Circuits

Project #5

Rocket Versus Asteroid

This project involved controlling a rocket to collide with an asteroid heading towards earth. The rocket was controlled using various control algortihms to account for different asteroid movement scenarios. This project required the rocket and the asteroid to be modelled before applying a controller to the rocket. It's always fun blowing stuff up, even if its just a simulation.

Matlab, Simulink

Project #6

Rowdy Chess V2

This project was a continuation of the Rowdy Chessboard you saw earlier (hence the name). We figured moving chess pieces around with a mechanical arm wasn't cool enough and decided to try make it happen using electromagnets instead. Unfortunately, this proved to be harder than anticipated and the project has been put on hold. We did design a pretty cool Printed Circuit Board that can detect when a piece is on a certain square on the board.

PCB Design, KiCAD, App Development, Python
