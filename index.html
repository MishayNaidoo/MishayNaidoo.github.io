About

I really hate writing exams.

Those where my exact thoughts the day I finished matric back in 2018. However, after taking a one year hiatus from exams while I pursued a degree in 3D animation and game design, I convinced myself that exams weren't so bad after all and enrolled in an engineering degree. Now, almost four years and roughly a trillion exams later I can once again confidently say:

I really hate writing exams...

Whilst my disdain for exams remains resolute, I have fallen in love with engineering. My lecturers in this degree seemed to have a knack for throwing projects and assignments at me that I was totally incapable of doing and proceeding to tell me to go ahead and do them anyway. This in turn, taught me how to approach projects that seemed impossible at the start with a confidence and belief that I could get them done. With this attitude, I have gone on to pursue and accomplish various projects that this website is here to show off to you. However, my blind confidence hasn't always been enough and there are one of two failed projects. Unfortunately, you'll read about those on here too.

In between all that engineering business, I have developed and furthered a few hobbies. Although I long since decided that a career in the field is not for me, I still enjoy playing video games and watching movies. I have also embraced the Cape Town attitude somewhat, and try to keep healthy by going to the gym or, more recently, playing basketball.

I hope I've given you some insight into who I am without boring you to death (I really tried to make this as interesting as possible) but now I encourage you to go forth and explore my portfolio.

Projects

Project #1

Rowdy Chess V1

An automated chessboard myself and two friends created, that uses a 3D printed XY plotter to pick up and move chesspieces. This board enabled players to make chess moves using only voice commands.

Python, CAD, 3D Printing

Project #2

Disaster Drones

Currently, four of us have entered into a competition hosted by Mathworks where each team must design a control algorithm to solve a maze using a line following drone. This is something we are currently working on as the tournament is still in progress.

MATLab, Simulink, Control Systems Engineering

Project #3

Perch Scale

A smart perch designed to weigh Yellow-Billed Hornbills in their natural habitat without causing any distrubances to their environment. This was designed to aid researchers at the FitzPatrick institute in their research of the birds.

3D Printing, CAD, Embedded Systems

Project #4

Line Following Robot

This project involved designing a control algorithm that solved a maze using a two wheeled robot. The algorithm was created in Matlab Simulink and run on a microcontroller.

Matlab, Simulink, Embedded Systems, Analog Circuits

Project #5

Rocket Versus Asteroid

A website built for Space Club OAU, a campus non-profit community aimed at bringing awareness and building STEM capacity among students and individuals within and without the University.

WordPress, HTML5, CSS3
